What Actually Happens During an Emsella Treatment: Your Complete Session Guide

April 15, 2026 • 8 min read • By Dr. Joshua Fritz, DC

The most common question we hear before a first Emsella appointment is: "What is it actually going to feel like?" Here's a complete, honest answer — from walking in the door to finishing your sixth session.

Most people considering Emsella have read that it "uses electromagnetic energy to contract pelvic floor muscles" — but that description doesn't tell you what it's actually like to sit in that chair. It doesn't tell you what the sensation is, whether it's uncomfortable, what to wear, or how you'll feel walking out afterward. This post covers all of it: the practical stuff nobody thinks to write down until they're staring at a waiting room chair wondering what they signed up for.

What Emsella Is (The One-Paragraph Version)

Emsella is a specialized chair that uses High-Intensity Focused Electromagnetic (HIFEM) technology to trigger rapid, involuntary pelvic floor contractions. In a single 28-minute session, it produces 11,200 contractions — a level of pelvic floor activation you simply cannot achieve through voluntary exercise. You sit fully clothed. There's no preparation required, no insertion of anything, and no downtime afterward. For a deeper look at how the technology works and the clinical evidence behind it, see the full Emsella therapy overview.

Before Your First Appointment

There's genuinely very little to do to prepare. No dietary restrictions, no need to fast, no special skin prep. A few practical things to know:

What to wear: Comfortable clothing works best. The electromagnetic field is focused on the pelvic area, so you don't need to worry about removing a bra or any upper-body clothing. What you do want to avoid is metal near the seat — specifically:

  • Belts with large metal buckles (leave it at home or remove it before sitting)
  • Jeans with heavy metal rivets at the seat (these won't cause any harm, but pressing against them for 28 minutes is uncomfortable — leggings or soft pants are more pleasant)
  • Anything in your back pockets — empty them out

Loose pants, leggings, yoga pants, or soft athletic wear are ideal. You stay fully clothed the entire time.

Your bladder: You don't need to empty it beforehand, though many people prefer to. Either way is fine.

Time: The treatment itself is 28 minutes. Your first appointment will include a brief intake and check-in, so plan for about 35-40 minutes total for visit one. Follow-up sessions are faster since the setup is already established.

Arriving — The Chair

The first thing most people notice when they see the Emsella chair is that it looks... completely ordinary. It's a padded chair, slightly taller than a standard seat, without any visible attachments, tubes, or equipment on the surface. The electromagnetic coil is built directly into the seat — there's nothing to see on the outside.

You sit centered on the chair, fully clothed, feet flat on the floor. Your provider will position you correctly — the exact positioning matters because the coil needs to align with your pelvic floor anatomy. They'll adjust your posture to make sure the treatment area is centered over the coil before starting.

The intensity starts low for first-timers and gets increased gradually as the session progresses and your provider confirms you're comfortable. You're always in control of the dial.

The First Few Minutes — What You'll Feel

At low intensity, the first sensation is a subtle tingling or buzzing in the pelvic floor area. It's mild enough that some people aren't sure whether they're actually feeling anything or just imagining it.

As the intensity increases, that changes. You start to feel your pelvic floor muscles contracting — rhythmically, involuntarily, in pulses you have no control over. The best description most patients land on is something like: "It feels like doing Kegels very fast, but you're not doing them — the chair is doing them for you."

That's accurate. Your muscles are contracting, but your brain isn't initiating it. The electromagnetic field is triggering the motor neurons directly. For most people, the strangeness of having muscles move without choosing to move them is the most surprising part — not any discomfort.

Some people describe the sensation as mild pressure, a "fluttering," or a deep pulsing feeling. It's unusual in a way that's hard to describe until you experience it. Most people's first reaction is something between surprise and amusement.

If the intensity is too high at any point, you ask your provider to reduce it. That's a completely normal request — many patients prefer to keep it lower, especially in early sessions — and lower intensity still produces results.[1]

The 28 Minutes

The session follows a rhythm: bursts of rapid contractions followed by brief rest intervals. It's structured this way intentionally, mimicking the work-and-rest pattern of an actual muscle workout. You'll notice the contractions speed up, hold at peak intensity, then ease off — and then repeat.

You're fully awake throughout. You can have a conversation, scroll your phone, read, or just zone out. There's nothing you need to do except sit.

By the midpoint of the session, most patients feel some muscle fatigue in the pelvic floor — the same vague "worked muscle" tiredness you'd feel after an exercise set. Not painful, just noticeable. By the end of the 28 minutes, that fatigue is more pronounced for some people.

One thing worth mentioning: the inner thigh muscles are anatomically connected to the pelvic floor, and some patients feel mild involvement there during the session. This is normal and not a sign that anything is wrong.

A small number of patients find the sensation uncomfortable at higher intensities and prefer to run the session at a lower level. That's a completely valid choice. The treatment still works.

Right After the Session

You stand up. You feel normal.

There's no recovery period, no grogginess, no residual soreness for most people. Some patients notice mild pelvic floor "tiredness" for a few hours — the same feeling as after a leg day at the gym, but in a different set of muscles. For most people, it's subtle enough that they'd forget about it if they weren't paying attention.

A few patients describe a slight heightened awareness of the pelvic floor area for a few hours after the session — not discomfort, just a sense that those muscles were recently activated. That fades on its own.

Zero downtime. You can return to work, exercise, and everything else immediately after walking out the door.

How Sensations Change Across 6 Sessions

The experience isn't the same from session one to session six. Here's what the arc typically looks like:

Sessions 1-2: The sensation feels completely foreign. You may find yourself laughing — it's genuinely funny and strange to have muscles contracting without you doing anything. The provider is still calibrating the intensity to your comfort level, so these sessions are partly about figuring out your baseline.

Sessions 3-4: Your body has adapted to the sensation. It's familiar now instead of startling. This is also when most patients start noticing the first real-world changes — fewer leaks, less urgency, less middle-of-the-night urgency. The pelvic floor is getting stronger, and you can feel it.[2]

Sessions 5-6: The session has become routine. The sensations are known and unsurprising. Most patients at this point feel meaningfully better than when they started — many feel significantly better. The muscles are responding.

The sessions are spaced twice a week over three weeks deliberately. The spacing allows for muscle recovery between sessions — the same reason you don't do heavy weight training every single day. The rest periods are part of the treatment.

When Do Results Show Up?

This is the question everyone really wants answered, so here's the honest picture:

First improvements: Many patients notice changes during the treatment course itself — typically around sessions 3-4. Fewer unexpected leaks, more time between urgent bathroom trips, better sense of control. It's often subtle at first.

Peak results: 4-6 weeks after completing the 6-session series. This timing surprises a lot of people. The session isn't the end of the process — it's the stimulus. The muscles continue strengthening during recovery in the weeks after your last appointment, and that's when the full benefit is realized.[3]

This is worth emphasizing: if you finish session 6 and feel "pretty good, but not 100% there yet," don't conclude that it didn't work. Wait 4-6 more weeks. Most patients see continued, sometimes significant improvement after the last session without doing anything else.

Long-term results: 95% of patients report significant improvement. Maintenance sessions every 6-12 months sustain those results long-term — one session, occasionally, is usually enough to preserve what the initial course built.

Common Questions and Concerns

"Is it safe with a metal IUD?" Yes. The electromagnetic field isn't strong enough at that depth to affect an IUD. That said, always disclose any metal implants to your provider before the session — it's a standard part of the intake process.

"What about hip replacements or other metal implants?" Metal implants located farther from the treatment area are generally fine, but should always be disclosed. Your provider will review your history and make a determination before proceeding.

"Can I do it while menstruating?" Yes. Menstruation is not a contraindication for Emsella.

"Is it safe if I've had a C-section?" Yes. C-section scars are not a contraindication.

"What if I'm claustrophobic?" Emsella doesn't involve any enclosed space — you're sitting in an open room in a chair. Claustrophobia is not an issue.

"Can I get treatment if I have a pacemaker?" No. Pacemakers are a contraindication for electromagnetic therapy. This will always be screened before your first session. If you have a pacemaker, Emsella is not appropriate for you.

The Takeaway

The thing that surprises most patients after their first session is how ordinary the experience is compared to what they imagined. There's no drama, no pain, no recovery. You sit in a chair for 28 minutes, feel your pelvic floor do a lot of work on your behalf, and walk out. The hardest part of the whole process isn't the treatment itself — it's usually just making the first appointment.

If you've been on the fence because you didn't know what to expect, now you do. It's genuinely not a big deal. And the results, for most patients, very much are.
